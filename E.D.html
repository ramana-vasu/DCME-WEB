UNIT-1 Engineering Drawing for LEARNING OUTCOMES

1.1. State the importance of drawing as an engineering communication medium

1.2. State the necessary of B.I.S. code of practice for Engineering Drawing.

1.3. Explain the linkages between Engineering drawing and other subjects of Mechanical engineering

UNIT-2 Engineering Drawing Instruments

2.1. Select the correct instruments to draw the different lines / curves.

2.2. Use correct grade of pencil to draw different types of lines and for different purposes

2.3. Select and use appropriate scales for a given application.

2.4. Identify different drawing sheet sizes as per I.S. and Standard lay-outs.

2.5. prepare title block as per B.I.S. specifications.

2.6. Identify the steps to be taken to keep the drawing clean and tidy.

UNIT-3 Free hand lettering& numbers

3.1. Write title using vertical lettering and numbers of 7mm, 10mm and 14mm height.

3.2. Write titles using sloping lettering and numbers of 7mm,10mm and 14mm height.

3.3. Select suitable sizes of lettering for different layouts and applications.

UNIT-4 Dimensioning practice

4.1. Acquaint with the conversation, notations, rules and methods of dimensioning in engineering drawing as per the B.I.S.

4.2. Dimensions a given drawing using standard notations and desired system of dimensioning.

UNIT-5 Geometric Constructions

5.1. practice the basic geometric constructions like 1) dividing a line into equal parts 2) exterior and interior tangent to the given two circles 3) tangent arcs to two given lines and arcs

5.2. Draw any regular polygon using general method when 1)side length is given 2) inscribing circle radius is given 3) describing circle radius is given

5.3. Draw the engineering curves like 1) involute 2) cycloid 3)helix

5.4. Identify the applications of the above construction in engineering practice.

UNIT-6 Projections of points, Lines plans& Auxiliary planes

6.1. Explain the basic principles of the orthographic projections.

6.2. Visualise and draw the projection of a point with respect to reference Planes(HP&VP).

6.3. Visualise and draw the projections of straight lines with respect to two references Planes

6.4. Visualise and draw the projections of planes (up to planes perpendicular to one plane and inclined to other plane)

6.5. Draw the auxiliary views of the given engineering components.

UNIT-7 Projections of Solids

7.1. visualise and draw the projections of regular solids like prisms, Pyramid, Cylinder,Cone...

UNIT-8 Sections of solid

8.1. Identify the need to draw sectional views.

8.2. Differentiate between true shape and apparent shape of section.

8.3. Draw sectional views and true sections of regular solids by applying the principles of hatching.

UNIT-9 orthographic projections

9.1. Draw the orthographic views of an object from its pictorial drawing.

9.2. Draw the minimum number of views needed to represent a given object fully.

UNIT-10 Isometric views

10.1. identify the need of isometric drawing.

10.2. Differentiate between isometric scale and true scale.

10.3. Prepare isometric views from the given orthographic drawing.

UNIT-11 Development of surfacs

11.1. State the need for preparing development drawing.

11.2. Draw the development of simple engineering objects and their truncations (cubes, cylinders, cones, pyramid)

11.3. Prepare development of surface of engineering components like 1) funnel 2)Tray
